---
question: "Can I save my work?"
order: 4
---

Yes — with opt-in encrypted persistence.

By default, PAI is amnesic: everything is wiped at shutdown. To save data across sessions, set up the encrypted persistence partition:

```bash
# First-time setup (creates LUKS partition on your USB)
sudo pai-persistence setup

# Save your data manually at any time
pai-save

# Data is also auto-saved on shutdown
```

What gets saved:

- Your home directory (`~/`)
- SSH keys (`~/.ssh/`)
- GPG keyring (`~/.gnupg/`)
- Electrum wallet (`~/.electrum/`)
- Downloaded AI models (so you don't re-download each session)
- PAI UI chat history and settings

The persistence partition is encrypted with LUKS2 (AES-256). At boot, PAI asks for your passphrase on a secondary terminal (tty2). If you skip it, PAI boots normally in amnesic mode.

**Your passphrase is the only key to your data. There is no recovery if you lose it.**
