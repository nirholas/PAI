---
question: "Why Debian and not Arch/Alpine?"
order: 9
---

Three reasons: `live-build`, stability, and ecosystem.

**`live-build`** is Debian's toolchain for building live ISOs. It's battle-tested by Tails, Kali, and Parrot. Building a live USB OS that boots reliably across hundreds of hardware configurations is hard — `live-build` solves most of that problem. Porting to Arch or Alpine would require rebuilding that entire toolchain from scratch.

**Stability.** Debian Stable (Bookworm) has predictable behavior: packages don't break between runs, the kernel is well-tested, and security patches come reliably for 5 years. Arch's rolling release is great for personal machines but harder to pin for a reproducible OS build.

**Ecosystem.** Debian has 59,000+ packages in its repositories. Tails, Kali, and Parrot all chose Debian for the same reasons. The live USB community tooling, documentation, and institutional knowledge is deeply Debian-oriented.

Alpine's tiny size is appealing but its musl libc introduces compatibility headaches with binaries like Ollama that are built against glibc.

Could PAI work on Arch or Alpine? Probably. Would it be worth the engineering cost compared to the user benefit? Not yet.
